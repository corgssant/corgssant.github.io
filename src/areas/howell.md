## Livingston County is part of our range

Howell is the Livingston County seat, and Livingston is one of the six counties where CNO Pole Barns works: Oakland, Genesee, Lapeer, Macomb, Livingston, and Washtenaw. Howell sits toward the western end of that range, farther from Clarkston than the home ground around Oakland County, so we plan the schedule around the drive rather than pretend the distance is not there. CNO has been building in Michigan since 1970 and is locally owned today by Zosim Serban.

## Working buildings for Livingston County

Livingston County keeps more farmland than the counties east of it, and the townships around Howell - Howell Township, Marion, Oceola, and Genoa - hold a mix of working farms, horse properties, and newer subdivisions. The steady requests are [pole barns](/services/pole-barns/) built for daily farm use: hay under cover, equipment out of the weather, a shop bay with a tall door. For larger ag or commercial spans, CNO is a certified Federal Steel Systems dealer and can quote, deliver, and stand up a [Federal Steel building](/steel-buildings/) instead of post-frame.

## City of Howell or one of four townships

A Howell mailing address does not tell you who reviews your permit. Inside the city limits, it is the City of Howell. Outside them, the parcel belongs to Howell Township, Marion, Oceola, or Genoa, and each township runs its own building department. We pin down the right office from your parcel information before we submit, and the plot plan and application come with the job.

## What a Howell job looks like

Same crews, same process as the jobs closer to Clarkston. The estimate is still free and still happens on your property, and owner Zosim Serban stays involved the same way he does on home ground. The drive is the only real difference, and we plan the schedule around it rather than letting it surprise you.
