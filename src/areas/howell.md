## A step west of our home counties

Howell is the Livingston County seat, and it sits just outside the four counties where CNO Pole Barns does most of its work: Oakland, Genesee, Lapeer, and Macomb. We take Howell-area jobs as a westward extension of that territory. It is not home ground the way Clarkston is, and we would rather say that plainly than pretend otherwise. CNO has been building in Michigan since 1970 and is locally owned today by Zosim Serban.

## Working buildings for Livingston County

Livingston County keeps more farmland than the counties east of it, and the townships around Howell - Howell Township, Marion, Oceola, and Genoa - hold a mix of working farms, horse properties, and newer subdivisions. The steady requests are [pole barns](/services/pole-barns/) built for daily farm use: hay under cover, equipment out of the weather, a shop bay with a tall door. For larger ag or commercial spans, CNO is a certified Federal Steel Systems dealer and can quote, deliver, and erect a [Federal Steel building](/steel-buildings/) instead of post-frame.

## City of Howell or one of four townships

A Howell mailing address does not tell you who reviews your permit. Inside the city limits, it is the City of Howell. Outside them, the parcel belongs to Howell Township, Marion, Oceola, or Genoa, and each township runs its own building department. We pin down the right office from your parcel information before we submit, and the plot plan and application come with the job.

## What a westward job looks like

Same crews, same process as our core counties. The estimate is still free and still happens on your property, and owner Zosim Serban stays involved the same way he does closer to home. Geography is the only real difference, and we plan the schedule around it rather than letting it surprise you.
