## Pole Barns for Holly's Rural Character

Holly, Michigan, sits at the northern edge of Oakland County where the landscape shifts from suburban to genuinely rural. Rolling hills, wooded acreages, and open farmland define the area — and that's exactly the kind of terrain where pole barns make the most sense. CNO Pole Barns, headquartered just 15 minutes south in Clarkston, has been building throughout the Holly area since 1970. We understand the needs of property owners in Holly Township and the village itself, from hobby farmers to equestrians to homeowners who simply need more space.

## A Natural Fit for the Holly Area

Holly's proximity to the Holly State Recreation Area and the Holdridge Lakes chain means many property owners here lead active, outdoor-oriented lifestyles. Our clients in the Holly area use their pole barns for everything — tractor and ATV storage, seasonal equipment, firewood processing, and full workshops for metalwork or auto restoration. The larger parcels common in Holly Township give us room to build substantial structures, often 40x60 or larger, with features like lean-tos, multiple overhead doors, and upper loft storage.

## Equestrian Construction in Holly

The Holly area is home to a thriving horse community, and we've built dozens of equestrian facilities in the surrounding townships. Our horse barns are designed with the animals' comfort and safety in mind — proper ventilation, durable stall partitions, rubber-matted floors, and wide aisle ways for easy handling. We can also build attached or detached hay storage, run-in shelters for pasture turnout, and covered riding arenas to extend your riding season through Michigan's long winters.
