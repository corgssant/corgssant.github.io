## Pre-Engineered Steel for Offices, Retail, and Community Buildings

A commercial steel building is a pre-engineered structure built around a bolt-together steel frame instead of studs or wood posts. The frame, panels, and trim get designed and cut at the factory, then shipped to your site ready to assemble. That's what lets a steel building span a wide open floor, whether it's a retail showroom, an office building, or a church or community hall that needs one big room without support columns breaking up the space.

CNO is a certified Federal Steel Systems dealer. We quote, deliver, and erect these buildings for customers across southeast Michigan, and we handle the whole job from the first site walk to the final punch list.

## Why go steel for a commercial building?

Steel frames give you clear spans up to 250 feet and bay widths up to 60 feet, so you get open floor space without rows of posts eating into your layout. The frames also carry point loads, so if you're planning rooftop HVAC or other equipment, the structure is built to take it from day one. Fire-resistant steel construction can lower your insurance premiums by up to 30 percent, which matters on a commercial policy. And cladding options can be finished to look like brick, stucco, or stone, so the building doesn't have to read as a metal box from the parking lot.

Panel and roof systems come in a range of R-values, so you can spec the building for year-round comfort rather than treating insulation as an afterthought.

## Steel or post-frame for your commercial project?

Steel isn't the right call for every commercial building. If your project is smaller, your site is tight, or wood framing fits the budget better, take a look at our [post-frame commercial buildings](/services/commercial-buildings/) instead.
